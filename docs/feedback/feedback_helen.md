## General

- Documentation  should use American English. Currently, there are some instances of British conventions being used (sub-projects hyphenated, organisation, authorisations, localisation, etc.)
- When step-by-step instructions are provided, they should be more specific. Each click, particularly for navigation, that a user makes should be included in these instructions. For example, to archive a person, it says "Open their profile and click Archive". But "Archive" is not immediately visible when you open a person's profile. You must click on the kebab menu icon to display the archive option. Or, to tag a person, it says "Open their profile and assign tags", but there is no explanation about where or how in the person's profile the tags are assigned.
- Throughout the documentation, instructions often include a step to "Click the **\[\+\]** icon". Instructions should always include the complete, accurate labeling seen in the tool, i.e., "Add Person", "Add Task", etc.

## Getting Started

- In the "Browse by topic" section, there is a card for "Projects and tasks" that links to the projects page. But tasks are not covered in the projects page. In cases like this, ensure that the copy accurately reflects that content actually included in that page.
- Also, in the "Browse by topic" section, the description on the "People" card is inaccurate. This page covers adding, inviting, and configuring users (not really setting up teams or companies)
- Also, in the "Browse by topic" section, the description on the "Planning" card is inaccurate. It mentions scheduling tools. There is a brief mention about scheduling, but it is not among the most relevant content for this page. It would be better to mention tasks.

### Quickstart

- At times, such as here on the Quickstart page, the introductory text in the body is superfluous and reiterates what is already explained in the subheading. When this occurs on a page, it is better not to include superfluous or redundant introductory text.
- The onboarding flow section is unnecessary. In almost every case, the user reading this page will have already gone through the onboarding flow.
- Instructions for creating a project are inaccurate. You do not assign a project to a category after creating it. The instructions should be specific and explain the distinction between adding a new category and adding a project to an existing category.
- The tooltip here ("Don't overthink your structure...") is somewhat contradictory to the tooltip on the project page about planning/mapping your hierarchy ahead of time.
- In the instructions to "Add your team", the button labels are incorrect. \[\+\] should be "Add Person," and "Send Invitation" should be "Invite by email". Ensure that instructions always include the exact text that appears in the tool.
- In the instructions to "Track your first time entry", the "Start Timer" option does not currently exist in the app. Again, button labels are inaccurate. In this case, rather than "Add" it should be "\[\+\]".
- The instructions to "Run a basic report" are, generally, inaccurate. Also, for the first report, it makes more sense to have users check the existing "Hours by person & project" report, while also offering a tip about organizing reports into folders and where those appear.
- On this page, the explanation of tags under "Organize your work with tags" is limited to projects. Tags can be used to categorize any entity, not just projects. While this is explained elsewhere in the documentation, it should also be stated more clearly here.

### Key Concepts

- This page contains another example of a superfluous introductory paragraph. When this occurs on a page, it is better not to include superfluous or redundant introductory text.
- A basic explanation of default role permissions should be included here (by default, employees have access to their own data, managers have access to their team's or project's data, admins have access to everything).

## Manage

### Projects

- Examples provided in the documentation should correspond with the dummy data that appears when you land in a new account. For example, for project categories, the examples given should be something like "Clients", "Internal Work", etc., with Client 1, Client 2, etc., on the project level.
- The instructions for creating projects and moving projects to another category are inaccurate. There is no "project tree" or drag-and-drop capabilities. You do not assign a project to a category after creating it. The instructions should be specific and explain the distinction between adding a new category and adding a project to an existing category.
- There is no "split by tags" option when configuring billing rates in a project. The options and settings for certain entity attributes may vary depending on whether it is a person, project, etc. Ensure that the specific options that appear are correct for the given entity.
- There is a section about "Timesheet settings per project", but these settings do not exist for projects in the tool.
- The "Show or hide" module for projects doesn't list absence types or tasks. Again, the options and settings for certain entity attributes may vary depending on whether it is a person, project, etc. In this particular case, these are options that appear in "Show or hide" for people, not for projects.
- Instructions for adding custom fields to projects are inaccurate. You do not click to "Add a custom field". Rather, the custom fields that are available for that project appear automatically, and you define the field data (the text, number, date, etc.) for that particular project.

### People

- This page contains another example of a superfluous introductory paragraph.
- The tooltip about roles at the top of this page feels out of place because there is a section about roles further down. Ensure that tooltips are always grouped by topic in the appropriate section on a page.
- Under "Schedule assignment", the "Attributes" section does not exist. These instructions and the labeling used are inaccurate. A person must click "Work schedule" and then select an existing schedule type from the menu. 
- The "Schedules, billing, and costs on people" section is redundant. These are all topics covered in more detail in other sections on the same page.

### Tags

- Tags are not located under settings. Tags exist on their own page in the left-hand menu.
- This page also mentions drag-and-drop functionalities that do not exist.

## Configuration

### Work Schedules

- Work schedules don't need to be weekly. While this is alluded to under "Schedule length," it really isn't clear. Other content on the page repeatedly insinuates that schedules are weekly. 
- The instructions to "Create a work schedule" are inaccurate and incomplete. They don't include any information about intervals, work-from-home options, or defining potential owners. Also, the click to save is done before defining the schedule details, not after.
- Under time period management, there is a reference to a non-existent "save" button. Most changes in Beebole are saved automatically; always verify when there is an exception.

### Custom Fields

- The instructions for "Creating a custom field" are confusing and out of order. The optional settings that appear after you select the field type are explained _after_ entity visibility. They should come before.
- Under "Creating a custom field", there is a reference to a non-existent "save" button. Most changes in Beebole are saved automatically; always verify when there is an exception.
- This page is missing an explanation of where and how the actual field data are defined on entities

### Roles & Authorizations

- From a user perspective, it would be more helpful to organize this page by permission type. In other words, sections about Approval Events, Billing Rates, Custom Fields, etc., with each section explaining the meaning or implications of that specific permission and the controls available for it. The page should still include sections for creating and managing roles, assigning roles to people, and best practices. 

### Assignments

- The instructions for default availability are not accurate. Not all items have a toggle on or off to make them available to everyone or no one. This exists for projects, but not for expense types or tasks, for example. 
- This page includes multiple references to a non-existent "save" button. Most changes in Beebole are saved automatically; always verify when there is an exception.
