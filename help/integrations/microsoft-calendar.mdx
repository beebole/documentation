Beebole's Microsoft Calendar integration lets you import your Outlook calendar events directly into your timesheet. Instead of manually entering time for meetings and appointments, you can view your Outlook events alongside your timesheet and drag them onto the correct project rows to log time instantly.

---

## How it works

Once connected, your Outlook calendar events appear in a panel next to your timesheet. You can drag and drop events onto timesheet rows to create time entries. Beebole automatically fills in the duration based on the calendar event length.

Events that have already been assigned to a timesheet row are marked as **Already tracked**, so you always know which events still need to be logged.

<Info>The Microsoft Calendar integration is a personal connection. Each user connects their own Outlook calendar from their timesheet view. Account administrators do not need to configure this centrally.</Info>

---

## Connecting your Outlook calendar

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your timesheet">
    Navigate to your **Timesheet** page in Beebole.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the calendar panel">
    Click the **Calendar** icon in the timesheet toolbar to open the calendar events panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect to Microsoft Calendar">
    Click **Connect to Microsoft Calendar**. A popup appears asking you to sign in to your Microsoft account and authorize Beebole to read your calendar events.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize access">
    Follow the Microsoft OAuth prompts to grant Beebole read-only access to your calendar. The popup closes automatically when the connection is complete.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View your events">
    Your Outlook calendar events for the current timesheet period now appear in the calendar panel. You can drag and drop them onto timesheet rows to log time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Using calendar events in your timesheet

Once connected, your workflow is straightforward:

1. Open your **Timesheet** and click the **Calendar** icon to show the events panel.
2. Your events for the current week (or day, depending on your timesheet view) are listed.
3. Drag an event onto the appropriate project row to create a time entry. The duration is filled in automatically.
4. Events already assigned to a row display an **Already tracked** indicator.

<Tip>If an event spans multiple projects, you can split it manually. Drag the event once, then adjust the duration and add another entry for the remaining time.</Tip>

---

## What data is imported

| Calendar data | Beebole behavior |
|---------------|------------------|
| **Event title** | Displayed in the calendar panel for reference. |
| **Event time and duration** | Used to pre-fill the time entry duration when dragged to a row. |
| **Event date** | Matched to the corresponding day in your timesheet. |
| **All-day events** | Displayed in the panel but do not pre-fill a duration. |

<Note>Beebole reads your Outlook calendar events in read-only mode. It does not create, modify, or delete any events in your Outlook calendar.</Note>

---

## Disconnecting Microsoft Calendar

To disconnect your Outlook calendar, click the **Calendar** icon in your timesheet toolbar, then click **Disconnect**. Your previously logged time entries remain in Beebole. Only the live calendar feed is removed.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can my administrator see my calendar events?">
    No. The Microsoft Calendar connection is personal to each user. Administrators cannot see your calendar events. They can only see the time entries you create in your timesheet.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does Beebole modify my Outlook calendar?">
    No. Beebole accesses your calendar in read-only mode. It cannot create, edit, or delete events in your Outlook calendar.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens if I update an event in Outlook?">
    Changes to your calendar events (time, title, deletion) are reflected in the Beebole calendar panel the next time you open it. However, time entries you have already created from a previous version of the event are not automatically updated.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I import events from shared Outlook calendars?">
    Beebole imports events from your primary Outlook calendar. Shared calendars or delegated calendars may not appear in the calendar panel.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need admin access to connect Microsoft Calendar?">
    No. Any Beebole user can connect their own Outlook calendar from their timesheet view. No administrator setup is required.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
