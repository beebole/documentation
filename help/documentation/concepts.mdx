Before diving into individual features, it helps to understand how Beebole is structured. This page introduces the core entities you'll work with and explains how features like billing rates, schedules, and custom fields connect across them.

---

## The core entities

Everything in Beebole revolves around four types of entities:

| Entity | What it represents | Examples |
|--------|--------------------|----------|
| **Projects** | The work your team tracks time against | Client engagements, internal initiatives, departments |
| **People** | The users and team members in your account | Employees, managers, contractors, administrators |
| **Tasks** | The activities you plan, schedule, and assign | Deliverables, milestones, work items |
| **Tags** | Organizational labels that group and classify entities | Departments, teams, locations, cost centers |

These four entities form the foundation of your Beebole account. Time entries, reports, budgets, planning, and approval workflows all build on top of them.

---

## How entities relate to each other

Beebole's entities don't exist in isolation — they connect to create a complete picture of your organization's work:

- **People work on projects.** When someone logs time, they select a project (and optionally a sub-project or task). This is the core relationship in Beebole.
- **Tags group both people and projects.** A tag like "Engineering" can contain both the engineering team members and the engineering projects, making it easy to filter and report by department.
- **Projects have hierarchies.** Categories contain projects, which can contain sub-projects, nested as deep as you need.
- **Tasks can be linked to projects.** Tasks are independent planning entities that can be associated with projects, connecting your planning and time tracking.
- **Tags have hierarchies too.** Tags can be organized into parent/child structures (e.g., "Europe" > "France" > "Paris office").

<Info>
  Think of projects as *what* your team tracks time against, tasks as *what you plan and schedule*, people as *who* does the work, and tags as *how you organize* everything for reporting and management.
</Info>

## Cross-cutting features

Several features in Beebole aren't tied to a single entity — they apply across projects, people, and tags. Understanding this pattern is key to configuring Beebole effectively.

### Billing and cost rates

Billing and cost rates can be set at multiple levels:

| Level | What it controls |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Organisation** | Default rates for the entire account |
| **Tag** | Rates for everyone and everything in a department or team |
| **Project** | Rates specific to a client or engagement |
| **Person** | Rates for an individual team member |

When multiple rates exist, Beebole uses a priority system: **person > project > tag > organisation**. The most specific rate always wins.

Learn more: [Billing rates](/help/documentation/billing) · [Cost rates](/help/documentation/costs)

### Work schedules

Work schedules define standard working hours per day of the week. Like rates, they can be assigned at the organisation, tag, or person level — and follow the same priority system.

Learn more: [Work schedules](/help/documentation/work-schedule)

### Custom fields

Custom fields let you add your own data to entities. You can create fields that appear on:
- **People** (e.g., Employee ID, Office Location)
- **Projects** (e.g., Priority, Contract Type)
- **Tasks** (e.g., Sprint, Story Points)
- **Time entries** (e.g., Activity Type, Deliverable)

Learn more: [Custom fields](/help/documentation/custom-fields)

### Assignments and availability

You control which projects, tasks, absence types, and expense types each person can access. These assignments can be managed at the organisation, tag, or individual level.

Learn more: [Assignments](/help/documentation/assignments)

## How it all comes together

Here's a typical setup to illustrate how these concepts connect:

1. **You create projects** organized by client (categories) and engagement (projects).
2. **You add people** and assign them roles that control their permissions.
3. **You create tags** for departments and teams, then tag both people and projects.
4. **You set billing rates** on projects (what you charge clients) and **cost rates** on people (what they cost internally). Tags can provide default rates for entire departments.
5. **You assign work schedules** to define expected working hours — at the org level for most people, with overrides on specific tags or individuals.
6. **People log time** against projects on their timesheets. Each entry captures who, what, when, and how long.
7. **Reports** combine all of this — showing time, costs, billing, and budgets across any combination of projects, people, and tags.

## Platform-wide features

A few features in Beebole apply globally, regardless of which section you're working in.

### Search

Each entity list in Beebole — people, projects, tasks, tags, roles, custom fields, absence types, schedules — has a **Search** field at the top of its panel. Beebole uses fuzzy matching, so typing characters that appear in order anywhere in an entity's name returns a hit even when they're not consecutive. Matched characters are highlighted in the results so you can confirm you've found the right entity.

Use the search bar to:

- **Find an entity by partial name.** Typing `alibr` matches "Alice Brooks" and "Aliso Bridges".
- **Filter long lists.** Searching narrows hierarchies like the project tree, expanding only the branches that contain matching entries.
- **Jump directly to a record.** Pressing **Enter** on a single match opens that entity's detail panel.

Clear the search field to restore the full list. Search is local to the panel you're in — there is no single search across every entity type, so use the [sidebar navigation](#how-it-all-comes-together) to switch entity types when you need to look elsewhere.

### Real-time sync

Beebole keeps everyone in your account in sync without requiring a refresh. When a teammate creates, edits, archives, or deletes anything — a project, a task, a time entry, an approval action, a journal message — the change appears for all other connected users within seconds.

Real-time sync covers:

- **Entity changes** — Adds, edits, archives, deletes, renames.
- **Approval actions** — Submitted, approved, rejected, and reopened timesheets update for the approver and the submitter at the same time.
- **Planning and Gantt updates** — Allocation changes propagate to the planning view and the Gantt chart in real time.
- **Journal messages and notifications** — New mentions, replies, and notifications appear without polling.

<Info>
  Real-time sync uses a persistent connection between your browser and Beebole. If your network drops temporarily, Beebole reconnects automatically and catches up on any changes you missed while offline.
</Info>

### Entity duplication

Most entities in Beebole support a **Duplicate** action that creates a copy with the same configuration as the original. Use it to bootstrap a new project from a template, set up a person record similar to an existing one, or clone a role with a small permission tweak instead of building it from scratch.

To duplicate an entity, right-click the entity name in its list — or open the **⋯** action menu that appears on hover — and select **Duplicate**. The new copy keeps:

- The source entity's settings, attributes, and configuration (rates, schedules, budgets, custom field values).
- Its position in the hierarchy (sub-project of the same parent, subtask of the same task, child tag of the same parent tag).

It does not copy historical data such as time entries, journal messages, or approvals — those are tied to the original entity.

<Note>
  Duplicating a person opens a small dialog where you enter the new person's **Name**, **Email**, and **Role** before the copy is created. This is because each person needs unique identity fields.
</Note>

### Attribute copy/paste

In addition to duplicating an entire entity, Beebole lets you copy a specific configuration block — such as billing rates, cost rates, work schedules, absence quotas, or budgets — from one entity and paste it onto another. Use it to keep configurations consistent across similar projects, transfer a person's quota structure to a new hire, or roll out a rate update to a group of related projects.

The basic flow:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the source entity">
    Open the entity whose configuration you want to copy (for example, a project with the right billing rate setup).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the attribute">
    Open the source's action menu and select **Copy**. Beebole stores the attribute configuration on the clipboard until you paste it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the target entity">
    Open the destination entity (a different project, person, or tag — whichever supports the same attribute).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste the attribute">
    Open the target's action menu and select **Paste**. The attribute configuration is applied to the target without affecting any other settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Attribute copy/paste is especially useful when onboarding new people or projects that should match an existing template. Set up one "model" entity correctly, then paste its rates, quotas, or budgets onto every new entity instead of recreating them by hand.
</Tip>

### Color coding

Beebole assigns each entity a color used as a visual identifier across the interface. Person avatars, project labels, allocation blocks, journal threads, and other entity references all share the same color, making it easy to scan a list, a calendar, or a Gantt chart and recognize who or what you're looking at.

The default color is generated automatically when an entity is created. Administrators and managers with the right permission can change the color manually:

- **For a person** — Open the person's profile in **People** and click the avatar to open the color picker. Pick a color from the palette, or remove the override to fall back to the auto-assigned color.
- **For other entities** — Color pickers appear next to the entity name in the detail pane wherever color coding applies.

<Info>
  Color is purely a visual aid — it does not affect access, permissions, billing, or reporting. Use it to make at-a-glance scanning easier, especially in shared views like planning, Gantt, or the journal.
</Info>

### Version update notifications

When a new version of Beebole is deployed, a banner appears in the interface with an **Update available** message. Click **Reload to update** to reload the app and apply the latest version. This ensures you always have access to new features and fixes without needing to clear your cache manually.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to set up all three entities before my team can track time?">
    You need at least one project and one person. Tags are optional but recommended — they unlock powerful reporting capabilities. See the [Quickstart](/help/documentation/quickstart) for the minimum setup.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What's the difference between a tag and a project category?">
    A project category is part of the project hierarchy — it groups projects together. A tag is a cross-cutting label that can be applied to both people and projects. Use categories for your project structure and tags for organizational dimensions like departments, locations, or cost centers.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="If I set a billing rate on a tag and a different rate on a project, which one applies?">
    The project rate takes priority. Beebole resolves rates in this order: person > project > tag > organisation. The most specific rate always wins.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I change my structure after my team has started tracking time?">
    Yes. You can reorganize projects, update tags, and change rates at any time. Historical time entries are preserved. However, structural changes may affect how existing data appears in filtered reports.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What's the difference between a sub-project and a task?">
    Sub-projects are part of the project hierarchy — they represent a subdivision of a larger project and are used for time tracking. Tasks are independent planning entities with their own properties like status, owner, dependencies, and effort tracking. Tasks can be linked to projects but exist separately, and are managed visually on the Gantt chart and Kanban board.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
