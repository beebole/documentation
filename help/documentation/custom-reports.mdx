Beebole's custom reports let you build multi-dimensional reports tailored to your exact needs. Choose which data to include, how to group it, and which filters to apply — then save your configuration as a reusable template you can run anytime.

<Info>
  Custom reports build on the same data as [built-in reports](/help/documentation/reports). The difference is that you control every aspect of the report layout and can save configurations for repeated use.
</Info>

---

## Creating a custom report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Reports page">
    Click **Reports** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new report">
    Click the **+** button or **New report** to open the report builder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a dimension">
    Select the type of record you want to analyze. Your choice determines the available columns and filters. See [Report dimensions](#report-dimensions) below.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add grouping">
    Choose how to group your data — by project, person, tag, or time period. You can add multiple grouping levels for multi-dimensional analysis.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select columns">
    Pick the data fields you want to display. Available columns depend on your chosen dimension and include time, financial, budget, and metadata fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply filters">
    Narrow the report to specific people, projects, tags, date ranges, or custom field values. See [Filtering your report](#filtering-your-report) below.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the report">
    Click **Run** to generate the report. Results appear in a table that you can sort by any column.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Report dimensions

The dimension defines what type of data your report analyzes. Each dimension gives you access to different columns and metrics.

| Dimension | Best for | Example use case |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Time records** | Analyzing hours, billing, and costs | "Show all billable hours by project for Q1" |
| **Expense records** | Reviewing project expenses | "List all travel expenses by person this year" |
| **Task records** | Examining task assignments and activity | "Show task completion across active projects" |

<Tip>
  Start with **Time records** if you are unsure — it is the most commonly used dimension and includes the widest range of financial and time data.
</Tip>

## Grouping options

Grouping determines how rows are organized in your report. You can combine multiple grouping levels to create multi-dimensional views.

### Group by entity

- **Project** — Group by project, category, or sub-project
- **Person** — Group by individual team members
- **Tag** — Group by tag for department or team-level views
- **Task** — Group by specific tasks within projects

### Group by time period

- **Year** — Annual totals
- **Quarter** — Quarterly breakdowns
- **Month** — Monthly summaries
- **Week** — Weekly details

<Tip>
  Multi-level grouping is powerful. For example, group by **Tag**, then by **Project**, then by **Month** to see how each department's projects perform over time.
</Tip>

## Selecting columns

Columns control what data appears in each row of your report. Available columns depend on your chosen dimension:

### Time and effort

- Hours, days, overtime hours
- Work-from-home indicator
- Approval status

### Financial

- Billing amount, billing rate
- Cost amount, cost rate
- Profit (billing minus cost)
- Margin percentage

### Budget

- Budget target (hours, billing, or cost)
- Budget consumed
- Budget remaining
- Budget percentage used

### Metadata

- Project code, project name
- Person name, person email
- Tag name
- Comments and notes
- [Custom field](/help/documentation/custom-fields) values

<Info>
  Custom fields you have created appear as available columns in the report builder. This lets you include data like project type, region, client reference, or any other attribute you track.
</Info>

## Filtering your report

Filters narrow the data included in your report. You can combine multiple filters for precise results.

| Filter | What it controls |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Date range** | Start and end date, or presets like "This month", "Last quarter", "Year to date" |
| **People** | Include or exclude specific team members |
| **Projects** | Filter by specific projects or categories |
| **Tags** | Include one or more tags |
| **Tasks** | Narrow to specific tasks |
| **Approval status** | Show only approved, pending, or rejected entries |
| **Custom fields** | Filter by any custom field value |

<Warning>
  Filters affect the data included in the report, not just the display. If you export a filtered report, only the filtered data is exported.
</Warning>

## Saving report templates

Once you have configured a report you want to reuse, save it as a template.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Configure your report">
    Set up the dimension, grouping, columns, and filters as described above.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the report">
    Click **Save** and give your report a descriptive name (e.g., "Monthly billing by project" or "Q1 team utilization").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign a category (optional)">
    Select or create a category to organize your saved reports. Categories help you group related templates together.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Saved reports preserve all your settings — dimension, grouping, columns, and filters. When you open a saved report, it runs with the latest data using your saved configuration.

<Tip>
  Use descriptive names that include the period and grouping, like "Weekly hours by person" or "Monthly billing by tag". This makes it easy to find the right report later.
</Tip>

## Organizing reports with categories

As your collection of saved reports grows, use categories to keep them organized.

- **Create categories** based on purpose (e.g., "Client billing", "Team utilization", "Budget monitoring") or audience (e.g., "Management reports", "Project manager reports").
- **Move reports** between categories as your needs evolve.
- **Rename or delete** categories at any time without affecting the reports inside them.

Categories are visible to all users who have access to the reports area, making it easy for your team to find the reports they need.

## Billing and cost attributes in reports

Custom reports give you full access to financial data alongside time data:

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Billing amount** | Revenue calculated from billing rates and tracked time |
| **Cost amount** | Internal cost calculated from cost rates and tracked time |
| **Profit** | Billing amount minus cost amount |
| **Margin** | Profit as a percentage of billing amount |
| **Budget consumed** | How much of the budget has been used |

For financial columns to show data, you need to have [billing rates](/help/documentation/billing) and/or [cost rates](/help/documentation/costs) configured on your projects, people, or tags.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I share saved reports with other users?">
    Saved reports are available to all users who have access to the reports area. Each user sees the same templates but only the data their permissions allow.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I edit a saved report?">
    Yes. Open the saved report, modify any settings (grouping, columns, filters), and click **Save** to update it. You can also use **Save as** to create a copy with different settings.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How many grouping levels can I use?">
    You can combine multiple grouping levels — for example, group by tag, then by project, then by month. There is no hard limit, but reports with many grouping levels can become complex to read.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do custom fields appear in reports automatically?">
    Yes. Any [custom field](/help/documentation/custom-fields) you create is automatically available as both a column and a filter in the report builder. You do not need to enable them separately.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I include both billing and cost data in the same report?">
    Yes. You can add billing, cost, profit, and margin columns to the same report. This gives you a complete financial view alongside time data. Make sure you have [billing rates](/help/documentation/billing) and [cost rates](/help/documentation/costs) configured.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
