Cost rates in Beebole track your internal cost of work — what it actually costs your organization when someone spends time on a project. Combined with [billing rates](/documentation/billing), cost data gives you a clear picture of profitability.

---

## How cost rates work

Cost rates follow the same structure as billing rates. They can be set at multiple levels, and the most specific rate always takes priority:

| Priority | Level | Example |
|----------|-------|---------|
| 1 (highest) | **Person** | A senior engineer with a higher cost rate |
| 2 | **Project** | A project with outsourced work at a fixed cost |
| 3 | **Tag** | A department-wide average cost |
| 4 (lowest) | **Organisation** | A company-wide default cost |

### Rate methods

Cost rates support the same methods as billing: **hourly**, **daily**, **fixed amount**, or **no cost**.

---

## Setting cost rates

### On a person

Open a person's profile in [People](/documentation/people) and configure their cost rate. This typically represents the person's loaded labor cost (salary + benefits + overhead).

### On a project

Open a project in [Projects](/documentation/projects) and set a project-level cost rate. Use **split by person** when team members have different cost rates on the same project.

### On a tag

Set a cost rate on a [tag](/documentation/tags) to apply a default cost to everyone in a department or team.

---

## Profitability analysis

When both billing and cost rates are configured, Beebole calculates profitability automatically in [Reports](/documentation/reports):

- **Billing amount** — Revenue generated
- **Cost amount** — Internal cost
- **Margin** — The difference between billing and cost

<Tip>
  Set up cost rates early, even if approximate. Having both billing and cost data from the start makes profitability reports immediately useful.
</Tip>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's the difference between billing rates and cost rates?">
    Billing rates represent what you charge (revenue). Cost rates represent what it costs you internally (expenses). The difference is your margin.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to set both billing and cost rates?">
    No. You can use either independently. Billing rates alone track revenue; cost rates alone track internal costs. Both together enable profitability analysis.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can cost rates have effective dates?">
    Yes. Like billing rates, cost rates support start and end dates for automatic rate changes over time.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
