Beebole's cost rates track your internal cost of work — what it actually costs your organization when someone spends time on a project. While [billing rates](/help/documentation/billing) represent what you charge, cost rates capture what you spend. Together, they give you a clear picture of profitability across your projects and teams.

---

## How cost rates work

Cost rates follow the same structure as billing rates. They can be set at multiple levels, and the most specific rate always takes priority:

| Priority | Level | Example |
|----------|-------|---------|
| 1 (highest) | **Person** | A senior engineer with a higher cost rate |
| 2 | **Task** | A task with outsourced work at a fixed cost |
| 3 | **Project** | A project with a specific cost allocation |
| 4 | **Tag** | A department-wide average cost |
| 5 (lowest) | **Organisation** | A company-wide default cost |

### Rate methods

Cost rates support the same methods as billing: **Hourly rate**, **Daily rate**, **Fixed fee**, or **No cost**.

---

## Setting cost rates

### On a person

Open a person's profile in [People](/help/documentation/people) and configure their cost rate. This typically represents the person's loaded labor cost (salary + benefits + overhead).

### On a project

Open a project in [Projects](/help/documentation/projects) and set a project-level cost rate. Use **Split by persons** when team members have different cost rates on the same project.

### On a tag

Set a cost rate on a [tag](/help/documentation/tags) to apply a default cost to everyone in a department or team.

---

## Rate splits

For projects where different team members have different internal costs, use rate splits:

- **Split by persons** — Set a specific cost rate for each person assigned to the project.
- **Split by projects** — When a tag-level cost rate needs per-project overrides.
- **Split by tags** — Apply different cost rates based on tags assigned to work.
- **No split** — One cost rate applies to everyone equally.

---

## Effective dates

Cost rates can have start and end dates so they change automatically over time. This is useful for:

- Annual salary adjustments
- Changes in overhead or benefit costs
- Temporary staffing at different cost levels

---

## Recurring rates

Recurring cost rates let you define a repeating rate pattern that applies automatically. This is useful when costs follow a predictable cycle — for example, quarterly adjustments to contractor rates.

You can set a rate to repeat **By day**, **By week**, **By month**, or **By year**. Select **Do not repeat** to disable recurrence on an existing recurring rate.

---

## Profitability analysis

When both billing and cost rates are configured, Beebole calculates profitability automatically in [Reports](/help/documentation/reports):

- **Billing amount** — Revenue generated
- **Cost amount** — Internal cost
- **Margin** — The difference between billing and cost

<Tip>
  Set up cost rates early, even if approximate. Having both billing and cost data from the start makes profitability reports immediately useful.
</Tip>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What's the difference between billing rates and cost rates?">
    In Beebole, billing rates represent what you charge (revenue). Cost rates represent what it costs you internally (expenses). The difference is your margin.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to set both billing and cost rates?">
    No. You can use either independently in Beebole. Billing rates alone track revenue; cost rates alone track internal costs. Both together enable profitability analysis.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can cost rates have effective dates?">
    Yes. Like billing rates, Beebole cost rates support start and end dates for automatic rate changes over time.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I set different cost rates per person on the same project?">
    Yes. In Beebole, use project-level **Split by persons** to assign a specific cost rate to each team member on that project.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
