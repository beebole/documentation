Beebole's billing rates let you track how much revenue your team's work generates. Billing rates can be configured at multiple levels — organisation, tag, project, or person — and Beebole automatically calculates billable amounts based on tracked time.

---

## Rate methods

Beebole supports several billing rate methods:

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Hourly** | A fixed amount per hour worked |
| **Daily** | A fixed amount per day worked |
| **Fixed amount** | A flat fee regardless of time spent |
| **Non-billable** | Mark time as non-billable (no revenue calculated) |

---

## Where to set billing rates

Billing rates can be defined at four levels. When multiple rates exist, Beebole uses the most specific one:

| Priority | Level | Use case |
|----------|-------|----------|
| 1 (highest) | **Person** | A contractor with a unique rate |
| 2 | **Task** | A specific task billed at a different rate |
| 3 | **Project** | A client project with a negotiated rate |
| 4 | **Tag** | A department-wide default rate |
| 5 (lowest) | **Organisation** | A company-wide default rate |

### On a person

Open a person's profile in [People](/help/documentation/people) and navigate to their billing settings. This rate applies to all time this person logs, unless overridden by a project-level rate.

### On a task

Open a project in [Projects](/help/documentation/projects) and navigate to its tasks. Set a billing rate on a specific task to override the project-level rate for that task only.

### On a project

Open a project in [Projects](/help/documentation/projects) and configure its billing rate. You can optionally **split by person** to set different rates for different team members on the same project.

### On a tag

Open a tag in [Tags](/help/documentation/tags) and set a billing rate. This rate applies to all people and projects within that tag, unless overridden at a more specific level.

### At the organisation level

Go to **Settings** > **Account** to define the default billing rate for your entire account. This serves as the fallback when no other rate is configured.

---

## Rate splits

For projects where different team members bill at different rates, use **rate splits**:

- **Split by person** — Set a specific rate for each person assigned to the project.
- **Split by project** — When a tag-level rate needs per-project overrides.
- **Flat** — One rate applies to everyone equally.

---

## Effective dates

Billing rates can have start and end dates, so rates change automatically over time. This is useful for:

- Annual rate increases
- Contract renewals with new terms
- Temporary promotional rates

---

## Recurring rates

Recurring rates let you define a repeating rate pattern that applies automatically over time. This is useful when rates follow a predictable cycle — for example, seasonal adjustments or periodic rate increases that repeat annually.

To set up a recurring rate, configure the rate with a start date and a recurrence interval. Beebole applies the rate pattern automatically for each cycle.

---

## Viewing billing data in reports

Billing amounts appear automatically in [Reports](/help/documentation/reports) when you include billing-related columns. You can group and filter by project, person, tag, or time period to analyze revenue from any angle.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What happens if no billing rate is set?">
    Time entries without a billing rate are treated as non-billable. They still appear in reports but with zero billing amount.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I mark individual time entries as non-billable?">
    Yes. Each time entry has a non-billable flag that overrides the default rate for that specific entry.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I set different rates for the same person on different projects?">
    Yes. Use project-level rate splits by person. The project rate takes priority over the person's default rate.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do recurring rates work?">
    Recurring rates repeat a rate pattern over time. This is useful for rates that follow a predictable cycle, such as seasonal adjustments.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I set a billing rate on a specific task?">
    Yes. Task-level billing rates override the project-level rate. This is useful when certain tasks within a project are billed at a different rate.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
