Welcome to Beebole. When you first create your account, Beebole walks you through a guided onboarding flow — you'll choose which features to activate (projects, tasks, schedules, absences, expenses), enter basic company information, and optionally request a demo.

This guide covers the five foundational steps to complete after that initial setup, so your team can start tracking time and getting value from reports.

<Tip>
  You can always enable or disable features later in **Settings** > **Account**. Start with what you need now and expand as your usage grows.
</Tip>

## Step 1: Create your first project

Everything in Beebole revolves around projects. A project is the bucket your team logs time against — it could be a client engagement, an internal initiative, or a recurring department task. Until you have at least one project, there's nothing to track.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Projects module">
    Click **Projects** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new project">
    Click the **[+]** icon at the top of the list. Enter a name for your project and, optionally, assign it to a category (useful for grouping multiple projects under a client or department).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. Your project is now available for time tracking.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Don't overthink your structure at this stage. Start with one or two projects, then expand once you've seen how your team tracks time. You can always reorganize later.
</Tip>

## Step 2: Organize your work with tags

Tags add a second dimension to your data. Where projects answer *what* your team is working on, tags answer *how* — the type of work, the client phase, the service category, or anything else that matters to your reporting.

For example, if multiple projects include both client meetings and deep-focus development work, a tag group like **Work Type** with tags like **Meeting** and **Development** lets you filter and compare those activities across all your projects in a single report.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open tag settings">
    Go to **Settings**, then click **Tags**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a tag group">
    Click **[+]** to add a new group. Give it a name that reflects the category of information it captures — for example, **Work Type**, **Department**, or **Delivery Phase**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add tags to the group">
    Within the group, add the individual tags your team will select when logging time (for example: **Meeting**, **Development**, **Review**).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Tags are optional per time entry by default. If you want to make a tag required for every submission, you can configure that in **Settings** > **Timesheet Settings**.
</Info>

## Step 3: Add your team

Beebole's data is only as useful as the people contributing to it. Adding your team members now means everyone can start logging time immediately — and you'll have real, attributable data to work with from day one.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the People module">
    Click **People** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a team member">
    Click the **[+]** icon and enter the person's name, email address, and role. Roles control what each person can see and do in Beebole — **Employee** is the right starting point for most team members.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send an invitation">
    Once the profile is saved, open it and click **Send Invitation**. Beebole emails the person a secure link to set their password and log in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you're adding a large team, use the **Import** option to paste names and emails directly from a spreadsheet — no need to enter each person individually.
</Tip>

<Note>
  Adding a person and inviting them are two separate actions. A person's profile exists in your account as soon as you save it, but they can't log in until you send the invitation.
</Note>

## Step 4: Track your first time entry

This is where Beebole delivers its core value. Logging that first time entry — whether manually or with the live timer — confirms your setup is working and gives you something concrete to report on.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your timesheet">
    Click **Timesheets** in the left sidebar. You'll see a grid view of the current week.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a project">
    Click **Add** to add a row to your timesheet, then choose the project you created in Step 1.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log your time">
    Enter hours directly in the grid for the relevant day — or click **Start Timer** to track time in real time as you work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply a tag">
    If you set up tags in Step 2, select the appropriate tag for this entry. This is what makes your reports useful later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Your entry saves automatically. You'll see the hours reflected in the timesheet grid immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 5: Run a basic report

With a project created, a team member added, and at least one time entry logged, you have everything you need to run your first report. This is the moment Beebole pays off — turning raw time entries into a clear picture of where your team's hours are going.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Reports">
    Click **Reports** in the left sidebar and select **Time** from the list of report types.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your date range">
    Use the date picker to select the period you want to analyze. For this first run, choose the current week or month.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Group your data">
    Use the **Group by** control to organize results by **Project**, **Person**, or **Tag** — whichever view is most useful right now.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and export">
    Scan the results to verify your data looks correct. When you're ready, click **Export** to download the report as a spreadsheet, or share it directly from Beebole.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Save any report configuration you find useful by clicking **Save report**. It will appear in your Reports sidebar so you can return to it with one click.
</Tip>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to set up tags before my team can start tracking time?">
    No. Tags are optional. Your team can log time against projects right away, and you can add tags later. That said, setting up at least one tag group before tracking begins makes your reports much more useful from the start — retroactively tagging entries isn't possible.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I add multiple people at once instead of one by one?">
    Yes. In the **People** module, use the **Import** option to paste a list of names and email addresses directly from a spreadsheet. Beebole processes the entire list at once.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What's the difference between a project and a category?">
    A category is a top-level grouping — typically a client name or department. Projects sit inside categories and represent the specific work being tracked. You can also add activities inside projects for even more granular breakdowns. None of these levels are required; use whichever depth makes sense for your team.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will my team members see each other's time entries?">
    By default, employees can only see their own timesheet. Managers can see the timesheets of people assigned to them. Admins have visibility across the entire account. You can fine-tune these permissions in **Settings** > **Roles**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a limit to how many projects or team members I can add during the trial?">
    Your trial gives you full access to Beebole with no feature restrictions. You can add as many projects and people as you need to properly evaluate the tool. Contact support@beebole.com if you have any questions about trial limits.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
