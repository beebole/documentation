Beebole's timesheet settings let you control exactly how your team records time. From choosing a duration format to enabling features like timers and work-from-home flags, these settings shape the timesheet experience for everyone in your account.

<Info>
  Timesheet settings are account-wide and apply to all users. Only administrators can change them. Go to **Settings** > **Time entry** to access these options.
</Info>

---

## Accessing timesheet settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Click **Settings** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Time entry settings">
    Select the **Time entry** section. All timesheet configuration options are listed here.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Default periodicity

Choose whether timesheets display as a **weekly** or **daily** view by default. This affects the layout your team sees when they open their timesheet.

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Weekly** | A grid showing Monday through Sunday (or your configured week start day). Best for teams that enter time at the end of each day or week. |
| **Daily** | A single-day view focused on one day at a time. Best for teams that track time throughout the day with start/end times. |

---

## Duration format

Configure how time values are displayed and entered across the account.

| Format | Example | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **Hours and minutes (hh:mm)** | `7:30` | Standard hours-and-minutes notation |
| **Decimal hours** | `7.5` | Hours expressed as a decimal number |
| **Days** | `1` or `0.5` | Time expressed as full or partial workdays |
| **Percentage** | `100%` | Time expressed as a percentage of the scheduled workday |

<Tip>
  Choose the format that matches how your team thinks about time. Consulting firms often prefer decimal hours for invoicing, while manufacturing or shift-based teams may prefer hh:mm.
</Tip>

---

## Time format: 12-hour or 24-hour

When using start/end time entry, choose between a **12-hour** (AM/PM) or **24-hour** clock format. This setting affects how times are displayed and entered throughout the application.

---

## Duration vs. start/end times

Choose whether your team enters time as:

- **Duration** -- A single value representing how long they worked (e.g., `3:00` or `3.0`).
- **Start and end times** -- The specific times they began and finished (e.g., `9:00` to `12:00`). Beebole calculates the duration automatically.

<Note>
  Start/end mode provides more granular data and is useful for teams that need to track exactly when work happens, not just how long it takes.
</Note>

## Feature toggles

Beebole lets you enable or disable specific timesheet features to keep the interface clean and relevant for your team.

| Feature | What it does |
|---------|--------------|
| **Timer** | Lets users start/stop a real-time timer on timesheet rows. See [Timesheets](/help/documentation/timesheets). |
| **Work from home** | Adds a toggle on time entries to flag remote work days. |
| **Non-billable flag** | Allows users to mark individual entries as non-billable. |
| **Comments** | Enables a comment field on each time entry. |
| **Row pinning** | Lets users pin frequently used project/task rows to their timesheet. |
| **Copy previous week** | Adds a button to duplicate last week's timesheet structure. |

<Tip>
  Disable features your team doesn't use to reduce visual clutter. You can always enable them later without losing any data.
</Tip>

## Auto-submit

Auto-submit automatically submits timesheets for [approval](/help/documentation/approval) after a configurable number of days. This ensures timesheets enter the approval pipeline even if a team member forgets to submit manually.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable auto-submit">
    In **Settings** > **Time entry**, toggle **Auto-submit** on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the delay">
    Choose the number of days after the end of a period before auto-submit triggers. For example, setting it to 3 means a weekly timesheet ending on Sunday is auto-submitted on Wednesday.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Auto-submitted timesheets may contain incomplete data. Pair auto-submit with [timesheet reminders](#timesheet-reminders) so team members are notified before the deadline.
</Warning>

## Timesheet reminders

Configure email reminders to notify team members when their timesheets are incomplete or not yet submitted.

| Setting | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Reminder day** | The day of the week when reminders are sent |
| **Reminder target** | Who receives the reminder: people with unfilled timesheets, unsubmitted timesheets, or both |

Reminders help reduce late submissions and ensure your approval pipeline stays on schedule.

---

## Entry rules and validation

Beebole can enforce rules on time entries to prevent errors:

- **Minimum/maximum hours per day** -- Set boundaries on how much time can be logged in a single day.
- **Required fields** -- Make comments or specific custom fields mandatory before saving.
- **Overtime warnings** -- Alert users when entries exceed their scheduled hours.

These rules are optional and can be adjusted to match your organization's policies.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I change the duration format after my team has started tracking time?">
    Yes. Changing the format updates how values are displayed going forward. Existing time data is preserved and converted to the new format automatically.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can different people use different duration formats?">
    No. The duration format is an account-wide setting that applies to all users. This ensures consistency across reports and approvals.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if I disable a feature like comments after people have used it?">
    Existing comments are preserved in the database and remain visible in reports. Users simply can no longer add new comments until the feature is re-enabled.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I set different rules for different teams?">
    Timesheet settings apply to the entire account. However, you can use [Assignments](/help/documentation/assignments) and [Tags](/help/documentation/tags) to control which projects and tasks are available to different teams.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I change the first day of the week?">
    Go to **Settings** > **Time entry** and update the **Week starts on** option. This changes the column order in the weekly timesheet view for all users.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
