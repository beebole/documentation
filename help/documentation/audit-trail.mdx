Beebole's audit trail records every create, update, and delete action in your account. You can see exactly who made a change, when it happened, and which fields were affected. This gives your organisation full transparency and accountability over its time tracking data.

<Info>
  Only administrators can access the audit trail.
</Info>

---

## What the audit trail captures

Every significant action in Beebole is logged automatically. The audit trail records:

| Detail | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Operation** | Whether the action was a create, update, or delete |
| **Person** | Who performed the action |
| **Timestamp** | When the action occurred |
| **Entity** | What was changed (e.g., a project, a time entry, a person) |
| **Field-level changes** | Exactly which fields changed, including the previous and new values |

This level of detail means you can trace any modification back to a specific person and moment in time.

---

## Viewing the audit trail

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the audit trail">
    Go to **Settings** > **Audit trail**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse recent activity">
    The audit trail displays a chronological list of changes, with the most recent entries first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by person">
    Use the person filter to view only changes made by a specific team member. This is useful when investigating a particular modification.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Understanding change details

Each audit trail entry shows a summary of what happened. Click on an entry to expand it and see the field-level details:

- **Field name** -- The specific attribute that changed (e.g., "Name", "Status", "Start date").
- **Previous value** -- What the field contained before the change.
- **New value** -- What the field was changed to.

This argument-level tracking lets you reconstruct exactly what changed and why, which is valuable for compliance, dispute resolution, and quality control.

<Tip>
  Use the audit trail to verify that timesheet corrections were made correctly, or to confirm who approved or rejected a submission.
</Tip>

---

## Last-edited indicators

In addition to the centralised audit trail, Beebole displays last-edited information directly on entities throughout the application. When you view a project, person, time entry, or other record, you can see who last modified it and when.

This gives you a quick reference without needing to open the full audit trail.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How far back does the audit trail go?">
    The Beebole audit trail retains records for the lifetime of your account. You can view historical changes going back to when your organisation was created.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I export the audit trail?">
    The audit trail is available for viewing within Beebole. For export needs, use the [API](/api/introduction) to query audit data programmatically.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does the audit trail record who viewed data, or only changes?">
    The audit trail records create, update, and delete actions only. It does not track who viewed or accessed data.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I see changes made by archived users?">
    Yes. Changes made by a person are retained in the audit trail even after that person is archived. The person's name still appears alongside their actions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Are bulk actions recorded individually?">
    Yes. If you perform a bulk update (e.g., archiving multiple people at once), each individual change is recorded as a separate entry in the audit trail.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
