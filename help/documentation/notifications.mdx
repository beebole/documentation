Beebole keeps your team informed through in-app and email notifications. Notifications alert you to approval actions, mentions, timesheet reminders, and other important events — so nothing slips through the cracks. Each person can customize their notification preferences, and administrators can configure organisation-wide settings like reminders and email templates.

<Info>
  Beebole's notification system is designed to reduce noise. Digest batching, customizable preferences, and one-click unsubscribe ensure your team gets the right information without email overload.
</Info>

---

## Notification types

Beebole sends notifications for the following events:

| Event | Description | Who receives it |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **Timesheet submitted** | A team member submits their timesheet for approval | The assigned approver |
| **Timesheet approved** | A timesheet is approved | The person who submitted it |
| **Timesheet rejected** | A timesheet is rejected or sent back for changes | The person who submitted it |
| **Mentions** | Someone @mentions you in a journal message | The mentioned person |
| **Timesheet reminders** | Automated reminders for unfilled or unsubmitted timesheets | People with incomplete timesheets |
| **Approval reminders** | Automated reminders for pending approval actions | Approvers with pending timesheets |
| **Team events** | New team members, schedule changes, and other organisational updates | Relevant team members and managers |

## Notification channels

### In-app notifications

Notifications appear in the **Journal** with unread indicators. Beebole displays a badge count for unread notifications on the Journal icon, and uses a "new" separator line to help you quickly spot what you have not seen yet.

### Email notifications

Beebole sends email notifications for important events. Each email includes relevant context — for example, an approval notification email includes a summary of the submitted timesheet with dates, hours, and projects.

<Info>
  You can approve or reject timesheets directly from the email notification without opening Beebole. Reply-to-journal functionality also lets you respond to messages via email.
</Info>

### Web push notifications

Beebole supports browser push notifications for real-time alerts. When enabled, you receive desktop notifications even when you are not actively using the Beebole tab.

## Digest batching

Instead of receiving individual emails for every event, Beebole can aggregate notifications into periodic digests. This reduces email volume while keeping you informed of all activity.

Digests group related events together — for example, multiple timesheet submissions from your team appear as a single summary email rather than separate messages for each submission.

## Configuring your notification preferences

Each person can customize which notifications they receive and how they receive them:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your profile settings">
    Click your profile picture or name in the top-right corner, then select **Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Notifications">
    Select the **Notifications** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your preferences">
    For each event type, toggle notifications on or off. Where available, choose between individual emails and digest batching.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your preferences">
    Click **Save**. Your preferences take effect immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Every notification email includes a one-click **Unsubscribe** link at the bottom. You can manage your preferences directly from your inbox without logging into Beebole.
</Tip>

## Timesheet reminders

Beebole can automatically remind team members to fill in and submit their timesheets. Administrators configure reminders at the organisation level:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open reminder settings">
    Go to **Settings** > **Notifications** (or **Settings** > **Reminders**, depending on your configuration).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable timesheet reminders">
    Toggle timesheet reminders on and configure the frequency — for example, daily or weekly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set trigger conditions">
    Define when reminders fire: after a timesheet has been incomplete for a configurable number of days, or when a submission deadline approaches.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the scope">
    Apply reminders to everyone in the organisation, or configure them per person or tag.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. Reminders are sent automatically according to your schedule.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Approval reminders

Separate from timesheet reminders, approval reminders notify approvers when timesheets are waiting for their action. This helps prevent bottlenecks in the [approval workflow](/help/documentation/approval).

Approval reminders are configured alongside timesheet reminders. You can set:

- **Frequency** — How often the reminder is sent while timesheets remain pending.
- **Delay** — How many days after submission before the first reminder is sent.

## Email templates

Administrators can customize the content of notification emails to match your organisation's tone and branding:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open email templates">
    Go to **Settings** > **Email Templates**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a template">
    Choose the notification type you want to customize (e.g., submission confirmation, approval notification, reminder).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit the template">
    Modify the subject line and body text. Use dynamic fields to insert data automatically — such as the person's name, project name, date range, or hours total.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview and save">
    Preview the template to verify the layout and dynamic fields. Click **Save** to activate it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Email template changes apply to all future notifications of that type. Previously sent emails are not affected.
</Note>

## Reliable delivery

Beebole uses an outbox queue to ensure notifications are delivered even when a temporary network or service issue occurs. When a notification fails to send, Beebole retries it automatically up to three times before marking it as permanently failed.

<Info>
  Retry handling is fully automatic — you do not need to take any action if a notification is delayed. Beebole will attempt redelivery in the background and deliver the notification once the issue resolves.
</Info>

## Notification history

Beebole keeps a personal notification history for each user. All sent notifications are stored against your profile and surfaced in the **Journal** feed, giving you a record of past approvals, mentions, and reminders.

- **Per-context read tracking** — each notification is individually tracked. Beebole records when you read it so unread counts stay accurate across sessions and devices.
- **Filterable feed** — you can filter the Journal by notification type (e.g., show only mentions or only approval events).
- **Paginated history** — older notifications load on demand as you scroll, so the full history remains accessible without slowing down the initial view.

---

## Unread counts and management

Beebole tracks unread notifications per person:

- The **Journal** icon in the sidebar displays a badge with the number of unread items.
- Opening the Journal and scrolling past a notification marks it as read.
- A "new" separator line distinguishes items you have not yet seen from older ones.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I turn off all email notifications?">
    Yes. In your notification preferences, disable email for each event type. You will still see notifications in the in-app Journal.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can an administrator configure notification settings for the whole team?">
    Administrators control organisation-wide settings like reminder frequency, trigger conditions, and email templates. Individual notification preferences — such as which events to receive — are managed by each person from their own profile.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I respond to approval notifications by email?">
    Yes. Beebole's approval notification emails include action buttons to approve or reject timesheets directly. You can also reply to journal messages via email.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What is the difference between individual notifications and digests?">
    Individual notifications send one email per event as it happens. Digests group multiple events into a single periodic summary email. Digests reduce inbox volume while still keeping you informed of all activity.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do notification preferences sync across devices?">
    Yes. Your notification preferences are stored in your Beebole profile and apply everywhere — web browser, mobile, and email. Changing preferences on one device updates them for all.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
