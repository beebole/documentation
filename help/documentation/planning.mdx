Beebole's planning tools help you allocate your team's time across projects and tasks before the work happens. You can see who is available, how much capacity each person has, and where scheduling conflicts exist — all in one visual interface. Planning in Beebole connects directly to your time tracking data, so you can compare what was planned against what actually happened.

<Info>
  Planning works alongside the task views ([Gantt chart](/help/documentation/gantt), [Kanban board](/help/documentation/kanban), and calendar). While those views focus on individual task management within a project, the planning view gives you a cross-project, team-wide perspective.
</Info>

---

## Accessing the planning view

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Planning">
    Click **Planning** in the left sidebar. The planning view opens, showing your team members along the left axis and time periods across the top.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the time range">
    Use the date controls at the top to set the period you want to plan. You can view by week, month, or a custom date range.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding the planning interface

The planning view is a grid-based interface designed for resource allocation:

- **Rows** — Each row represents a person on your team. You can expand a row to see the individual tasks and projects assigned to that person.
- **Columns** — Each column represents a time period (day, week, or month, depending on your zoom level).
- **Allocation blocks** — Colored blocks in the grid show how each person's time is allocated. Each block corresponds to a task or project assignment with a scheduled effort.
- **Capacity bar** — A summary bar at the top of each person's row shows their total allocated percentage versus their available capacity.

<Tip>
  Switch between daily, weekly, and monthly views to plan at different levels of detail. Use the weekly view for sprint-level planning and the monthly view for long-term resource allocation.
</Tip>

## Allocating resources

You assign people to tasks and projects with a specific effort percentage for a given time period. This is the core action in the planning view.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a cell">
    Click on the intersection of a person's row and a time period column. An allocation form opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the project and task">
    Select the **Project** and, optionally, a specific **Task** within that project. This links the allocation to a task that also appears in the [Gantt chart](/help/documentation/gantt) and [Kanban board](/help/documentation/kanban).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the effort">
    Enter the **Effort** as a percentage of the person's available time. For example, 50% means the person is planned to spend half of their working hours on this assignment during the selected period.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the allocation">
    Click **Save**. The allocation block appears in the grid, and the person's capacity bar updates to reflect the new commitment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Editing an allocation

Click any existing allocation block to modify the project, task, effort percentage, or time period. You can also drag the edges of an allocation block to extend or shorten its duration.

### Removing an allocation

Click an allocation block and select **Remove** to delete it. The person's capacity is freed up immediately.

## Capacity planning

Capacity planning in Beebole lets you compare how much time your team is scheduled to work versus how much time is actually available. This helps you avoid overbooking and identify underutilized team members.

### How capacity is calculated

Beebole calculates each person's available capacity based on their [work schedule](/help/documentation/work-schedule) and any approved [time off](/help/documentation/timeoff). The planning view then compares this available capacity against the total effort allocated.

| Indicator | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Under capacity** | The person has unallocated time available during the period |
| **At capacity** | The person's allocations match their available hours |
| **Over capacity** | The person is allocated more time than they have available — a scheduling conflict |

<Warning>
  Over-capacity situations are highlighted in the planning view. While Beebole does not block you from over-allocating, these warnings help you rebalance workloads before they become a problem.
</Warning>

### Comparing planned vs. actual hours

One of the key benefits of planning in Beebole is the ability to compare planned effort against actual time tracked. The planning view can display:

- **Planned hours** — Based on the effort percentages you set in the planning grid.
- **Actual hours** — Based on timesheet entries your team has logged. See [Timesheets](/help/documentation/timesheets).
- **Variance** — The difference between planned and actual, helping you adjust future plans.

<Info>
  Planned vs. actual comparisons are most useful after a time period has passed. Use them in retrospective reviews to improve your future planning accuracy.
</Info>

## Workload visualization

The planning view includes visual indicators that help you quickly assess team workload distribution:

- **Color coding** — Allocation blocks are color-coded by project, making it easy to see how a person's time is split across different work.
- **Capacity bars** — Each person's row includes a progress bar showing their total allocation as a percentage of available capacity. Green indicates under capacity, yellow indicates near capacity, and red indicates over capacity.
- **Team summary** — A summary row at the top aggregates the team's overall capacity utilization for the selected period.

<Tip>
  Use the workload visualization to spot imbalances. If one person is consistently over capacity while another is under capacity, you can drag allocations between rows to rebalance the workload.
</Tip>

## Filtering and grouping

You can customize the planning view to focus on specific subsets of your team or projects:

- **Filter by person** — Show only specific team members.
- **Filter by project** — Show only allocations for selected projects.
- **Filter by tag** — Narrow the view to people or projects with specific tags. See below and [Tags](/help/documentation/tags).
- **Group by team** — Organize rows by team or department for a structured overview.

### Filtering tasks by tag

Tags give you a flexible way to label tasks with cross-cutting metadata such as priority, client, or team. You can filter the task list by tag to see only the tasks that match a given tag value.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the filter bar">
    Click **Filters** or **Show filters** above the task list. The filter bar expands.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a tag filter">
    Click **Add filter** and select **Task tag** from the filter type list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the tag value">
    Select the tag you want to filter by from the tag picker. The task list updates immediately to show only tasks that have that tag.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Combine filters">
    Add more filters to narrow the list further. Filters are combined with AND logic by default — a task must match all active filters to appear.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can also filter by **Person tag** or **Project tag** to narrow the view by tags applied to people or projects rather than tasks themselves.
</Tip>

## Recurring tasks

You can set a task to repeat automatically on a fixed schedule. Beebole generates future occurrences based on the recurrence rule you define, so you do not need to create repetitive tasks manually.

### Setting a recurrence rule

Open a task's detail panel and scroll to the **Repeat** toggle. Click it to enable recurrence, then configure the rule:

1. **Mode** — Choose **Calendar** to repeat on fixed calendar dates, or **After completion** to start the next occurrence only after the current one is marked done.
2. **Frequency** — Set how often the task repeats using the **Every** field combined with a period: **day**, **workday**, **week**, **month**, or **year**.
3. **Weekly pattern** — When repeating by week, select the specific days of the week (Mon–Sun) on which the task should occur.
4. **Monthly pattern** — When repeating by month, choose whether to repeat on the same day of the month, the same weekday of the month, the first day, or the last day.
5. **Holiday behavior** — When repeating by workday, choose what happens when an occurrence falls on a holiday: **Skip**, **Shift forward**, or **Shift backward**.
6. **End condition** — Toggle **End** on to set a stop date or a maximum number of occurrences (**or after N times**). Leave it off for an open-ended series.

Click **Show next occurrences** to preview the upcoming schedule before saving.

### Editing a recurring task

When you edit, move, or resize a recurring task occurrence, Beebole asks which occurrences to update:

| Option | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **This occurrence only** | Changes only the selected occurrence |
| **This and all future** | Splits the series from this point onward |
| **All occurrences** | Updates the entire series |
| **Skip this occurrence** | Hides this occurrence without affecting others |

### Deleting a recurring task

When you delete a recurring task, Beebole asks whether to delete **This task only** (removes the task and its virtual occurrences, any already-materialized exceptions become standalone tasks) or the **Entire series** (deletes the task and all related tasks in the series).

<Warning>
  Deleting the entire series is permanent and removes all tasks in the series. Beebole shows a count of the tasks that will be deleted before you confirm.
</Warning>

## Moving a task between categories

Tasks are organized under categories (the top-level groupings in your task plan). You can reassign a root-level task — along with all its subtasks — to a different category using the context menu.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the task context menu">
    Right-click the task name in the task list, or click the **⋯** action menu button that appears on hover. The task must be a root-level task (not a subtask) — only top-level items can be moved to a different category.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Change category">
    Click **Change category** from the menu. A submenu lists all other available categories.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the destination category">
    Click the target category name. The task and all its subtasks move to that category immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Subtasks cannot be moved to a different category directly — only root-level tasks support the **Change category** action. To move a subtask, first promote it to a root-level task by removing its parent relationship, then move it.
</Info>

## Hierarchical tasks

Tasks support a multi-level hierarchy of subtasks. You can nest tasks as deeply as your workflow requires, directly within any task view (Gantt, Kanban, or planning).

### Creating a subtask

There are two ways to add a subtask:

- **Hover method** — Hover over a task name in the task list. Click the **Add a sub task** icon (a small plus with an indent indicator) that appears on the right. A new subtask is created directly under that task.
- **Keyboard method** — Select a task using keyboard navigation (Arrow keys), then press **Tab** to make it a subtask of the task immediately above it in the list. Press **Shift+Tab** to move it back up one level.

### Expanding and collapsing

Click the chevron icon to the left of a task name to expand or collapse its subtasks. When a task is collapsed, its subtasks are hidden from the list but remain visible on the timeline.

Press **⌘+Arrow Right** to expand the selected task and move into its first child. Press **⌘+Arrow Left** to collapse the selected group.

### Working with subtasks across views

Subtasks created in one view are immediately visible in all other task views. The Gantt chart shows the hierarchy visually with indentation and an expand/collapse control. The Kanban board shows subtasks as individual cards in their respective status columns.

## How planning connects to other features

Beebole's planning tools integrate with several other parts of the application:

| Feature | Connection |
|---------|-----------|
| [Gantt chart](/help/documentation/gantt) | Tasks with dates and effort set in planning appear on the Gantt timeline |
| [Kanban board](/help/documentation/kanban) | Task status changes on the Kanban board are reflected in planning |
| [Timesheets](/help/documentation/timesheets) | Actual hours logged in timesheets are compared against planned effort |
| [Work schedules](/help/documentation/work-schedule) | Available capacity is derived from each person's work schedule |
| [Time off](/help/documentation/timeoff) | Approved leave reduces a person's available capacity in the planning view |
| [Budgets](/help/documentation/budgets) | Planned effort can be compared against project budgets |

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I need to plan every task for planning to be useful?">
    No. You can use the planning view to allocate high-level effort to projects without breaking it down into individual tasks. Detailed task-level planning is optional and depends on your team's needs.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if someone's work schedule changes after I plan their time?">
    Beebole recalculates their available capacity based on the updated [work schedule](/help/documentation/work-schedule). If the new schedule reduces their hours, previously at-capacity allocations may show as over capacity.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I plan time for someone who has not been added to a project?">
    No. The person must be assigned to the project first. You can manage project assignments from the project settings page. See [Projects](/help/documentation/projects).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How far ahead can I plan?">
    There is no limit to the planning horizon. You can plan weeks, months, or even quarters ahead. However, longer-term plans may need more frequent adjustments as priorities change.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can managers see only their team's planning?">
    Yes. Visibility in the planning view respects [Roles & permissions](/help/documentation/roles-authorisations). Managers see only the people and projects they have access to.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I set a task to repeat every two weeks?">
    Yes. Enable **Repeat** on the task, set **Every** to 2 and select **week** as the period. You can also choose which days of the week the task should occur.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I move a subtask to a different category?">
    Only root-level tasks support the **Change category** action. To move a subtask, first remove its parent relationship to make it a root-level task, then use **Change category** from its context menu.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How deep can I nest subtasks?">
    There is no hard limit on subtask nesting depth. For readability, most teams keep hierarchies to two or three levels.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I filter tasks by multiple tags at once?">
    Yes. Add multiple **Task tag** filters in the filter bar. By default, tasks must match all active filters (AND logic). You can change the logic to OR by adjusting the filter operator in the filter row.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
