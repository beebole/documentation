Beebole's role-based access control lets you define exactly what each person can see and do. Every person in your account is assigned a role, and each role is a collection of permissions across more than 24 scopes — from time tracking and project management to billing, expenses, and custom fields. This gives you fine-grained control over who accesses what.

<Info>
  Beebole includes default roles (Administrator, Manager, Employee) that cover common setups. You can use them as-is or create custom roles tailored to your organisation's structure.
</Info>

---

## How roles work <Badge>Admin only</Badge>

A role is a named set of permissions. Each person has exactly one role, and that role determines:

- Which pages and features they can access
- Whether they can view or manage data
- What scope of data they can reach (their own, their team's, or the entire organisation's)

When you change a role's permissions, the change applies immediately to everyone assigned that role.

---

## Creating and managing roles <Badge>Admin only</Badge>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Roles page">
    Go to **Settings** > **Roles**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new role">
    Click the **[+]** button. Enter a **Name** for the role (e.g., "Project Manager", "Finance Viewer", "Team Lead").
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure permissions">
    For each authorisation scope, choose the permission level. See the sections below for details on scopes and targets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the role">
    Click **Save**. The role is now available for assignment to people.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Start with one of the default roles and duplicate it to create variations. This is faster than building a new role from scratch and helps you avoid accidentally missing a scope.
</Tip>

## Authorisation scopes <Badge>Admin only</Badge>

Beebole organises permissions into more than 24 scopes, each covering a specific area of the application. Here are the main scope categories:

| Scope category | What it controls |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Admin** | Account-wide settings, subscription, and configuration |
| **Organisation** | Organisation structure, companies, and global preferences |
| **Staff / People** | Viewing and managing people profiles, invitations, and personal data |
| **Projects** | Creating, editing, and archiving projects and sub-projects |
| **Tasks** | Managing tasks within projects |
| **Time tracking** | Viewing and editing time entries on timesheets |
| **Absences** | Managing time-off requests, leave types, and quotas |
| **Expenses** | Viewing and managing expense entries |
| **Custom fields** | Accessing and editing custom field values |
| **Budgets** | Viewing and managing project and task budgets |
| **Billing / Costs** | Accessing billing rates, cost rates, and financial data |
| **Messages / Journal** | Reading and writing journal messages |
| **Schedules** | Viewing and managing work schedules |
| **Email templates** | Editing notification email templates |

## Permission targets <Badge>Admin only</Badge>

For most scopes, you choose not only the action (view or manage) but also the target — the range of data the permission covers:

| Target | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Me only** | The person can access only their own data |
| **My people** | The person can access data for people they manage (directly or via tags) |
| **My projects** | The person can access data for projects they are assigned to as manager |
| **Organisation** | The person can access all data across the entire organisation |

<Info>
  Combining scope and target gives you precise control. For example, a team leader might have **Time tracking — Manage — My people** (can edit their team's timesheets) but only **Billing — View — My projects** (can see billing rates on their projects, but not change them).
</Info>

---

## View vs. manage permissions <Badge>Admin only</Badge>

Each scope offers two levels of access:

- **View** — The person can see the data but cannot create, edit, or delete it.
- **Manage** — The person can view, create, edit, and delete data within the scope.

Setting a scope to **None** hides it entirely — the person does not see that area of the application.

| Level | Can see | Can create/edit/delete |
|-------|---------|----------------------|
| **None** | No | No |
| **View** | Yes | No |
| **Manage** | Yes | Yes |

## Field-level permissions <Badge>Admin only</Badge>

Some permissions go beyond page-level access and control visibility of specific fields. For example:

- **Billing rates** can be hidden from people who do not need to see financial data.
- **Cost rates** can be restricted so only finance or admin roles see internal costs.
- **Personal information** fields (phone number, address) can be limited to HR or admin roles.

When a field is hidden by permissions, it does not appear on the interface at all — there is no indication that the field exists.

<Warning>
  Review field-level permissions carefully when creating roles. A person who cannot see billing rates will not see rate columns in reports, on timesheets, or on project settings — which may affect their ability to do their job if they need that information.
</Warning>

## Default roles <Badge>Admin only</Badge>

Beebole ships with three built-in roles:

| Role | Typical use |
|------|------------|
| **Administrator** | Full access to everything. At least one person must have this role. |
| **Manager** | Can manage their team's timesheets, view projects, and run reports for their scope. |
| **Employee** | Can track their own time, submit timesheets, and view their own data. |

You can modify the permissions of default roles or create additional roles as needed.

## Assigning roles to people <Badge>Admin only</Badge>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the person's profile">
    Go to **People** and click the person's name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a role">
    In the **Role** dropdown, choose the role you want to assign.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The person's access updates immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Changing a person's role takes effect immediately. If you reduce their permissions, features and data they previously accessed become hidden right away.
</Note>

## Availability controls <Badge>Admin only</Badge>

Availability controls determine which projects, absence types, expense types, tasks, and custom fields are enabled by default for everyone in your organisation. Admins set the global defaults in account settings, and those defaults can then be overridden for individual tags, people, or projects.

### How it works

- **Global default** — Go to **Settings** > **Account** and look for the **Show or hide by default** section. Toggle each element type on or off for the entire organisation.
- **Override per tag** — Open a tag's settings and adjust its **Show or hide** attribute to make specific element types available or hidden for everyone in that tag, regardless of the global default.
- **Override per person** — Open a person's profile and adjust their **Show or hide** attribute to give that individual a different set of defaults.
- **Override per project** — Open a project's settings to control whether it appears in the project selector for people who have not been explicitly assigned to it.

The following element types have availability controls:

| Element | What it controls |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Show all projects** | Whether all projects appear by default in the project selector |
| **Show all secondary projects** | Whether sub-projects are shown alongside primary projects |
| **Show all time off** | Whether all absence types appear by default in the timesheet |
| **Show all expenses** | Whether all expense types appear by default |
| **Show all tasks** | Whether tasks appear in the timesheet by default |
| **Show all custom fields** | Whether custom fields are visible by default |

<Info>
  The availability system uses a cascade: the organisation default applies first, then the tag setting, then the individual person or project override. A more specific setting always wins over a more general one.
</Info>

<Tip>
  Hold **⌘** (Mac) or **Ctrl** (Windows) while toggling any availability checkbox to apply the change to all element types at once.
</Tip>

## Best practices <Badge>Admin only</Badge>

- **Principle of least privilege** — Give each role only the permissions it needs. Start restrictive and add permissions as needed.
- **Use descriptive role names** — Names like "Finance Viewer" or "Project Lead" make it clear what the role is for.
- **Audit regularly** — Review roles periodically to ensure permissions still match your team structure, especially after organisational changes.
- **Test before deploying** — After creating a new role, assign it to a test person and verify they see the correct pages and data.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can one person have multiple roles?">
    No. Each person is assigned exactly one role. If someone needs a combination of permissions, create a custom role that includes everything they need.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if I delete a role that people are using?">
    You cannot delete a role that is currently assigned to people. Reassign those people to a different role first, then delete the unused role.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I restrict access to specific projects?">
    Role permissions control the scope of access (me, my people, my projects, or organisation). For project-specific access, combine role permissions with [assignments](/help/documentation/assignments) to control which projects appear for each person.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do permission changes take effect immediately?">
    Yes. When you save changes to a role, every person assigned to that role sees the updated access immediately — no logout or refresh required.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a minimum role I must keep?">
    Yes. At least one person in your account must have the **Administrator** role to ensure account management is always possible. Beebole prevents you from removing the last administrator.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
