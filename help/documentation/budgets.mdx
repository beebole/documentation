Beebole's budgets let you set targets for billing, costs, or hours on your projects — and track progress against those targets in real time. Budgets help you spot overruns before they become problems.

---

## Budget types

You can set three types of budgets on a project:

| Type | What it tracks | Example |
|------|---------------|---------|
| **Billing budget** | Revenue target | "This project should generate €50,000" |
| **Cost budget** | Internal cost limit | "Don't spend more than €30,000 on this project" |
| **Hours budget** | Time allocation | "This project has a 500-hour cap" |

You can use any combination of these on the same project.

---

## Setting up a budget

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the project">
    Navigate to [Projects](/help/documentation/projects) and click the project you want to budget.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the budget settings">
    In the project settings, find the **Budget** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Define the budget">
    Enter your target amount for billing, cost, and/or hours.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. Beebole starts tracking progress against this budget immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Budget splits

For more detailed tracking, you can split a project budget by:

- **Person** — Allocate portions of the budget to specific team members.
- **Sub-project** — Distribute the budget across sub-projects within the hierarchy.

Budget splits let you see not just whether the overall project is on track, but whether individual contributors or work streams are within their allocation.

---

## Monitoring budgets

Budget progress appears in [Reports](/help/documentation/reports). You can view:

- **Budget amount** — Your target
- **Actual amount** — What has been spent/billed/logged so far
- **Remaining** — How much budget is left
- **Percentage used** — At-a-glance progress

<Warning>
  Beebole tracks budget consumption in real time, but does not block time entries when a budget is exceeded. Use reports to monitor progress and take action proactively.
</Warning>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I set budgets on categories (not just projects)?">
    Budgets are set on individual projects or sub-projects. For a category-level view, run a report grouped by category and include budget columns.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does Beebole alert me when a budget is exceeded?">
    Budget consumption is visible in reports and project settings. You can monitor progress proactively through saved reports.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I change a budget after it's been set?">
    Yes. You can update budget amounts at any time. Historical tracking is preserved — the report will reflect the current target.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do expenses count toward project budgets?">
    Yes. When cost budgets are configured on a project, expense amounts contribute to the budget consumption alongside labor costs.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
